Raspberry Pi’s, Makey Makeys, Arduinos and other Small Computing Devices

A Raspberry Pi “is a low cost, credit-card sized computer that plugs into a computer monitor or TV, and uses a standard keyboard and mouse.”
A Makey Makey “is an invention kit for the 21stcentury. Turn everyday objects into touchpads and combine them with the internet. It’s a simple Invention Kit for Beginners and Experts doing art, engineering, and everything in between.”
An Arduino “is an open-source platform used for building electronics projects. Arduino consists of both a physical programmable circuit board (often referred to as a microcontroller) and a piece of software, or IDE (Integrated Development Environment) that runs on your computer, used to write and upload computer code to the physical board”.
These small computing devices and other mini PCs can be used for many things: as a mini desktop, a tv set top box, a VPN or even use it to make a cup of tea! There are many tutorials online on different types of projects that can be made with small computing devices.
The future of mini computers and other small computing devices is bright, as the technology will continue to improve. With the declining PC sales in recent years, the increasing popularity of the mini PC could breathe new life back into the PC market. The introduction of the mini PC has combined the portability of a laptop with the processing power of a tower PC, all at the small size of a deck of cards.
The partial customisation of the mini PC may not currently compete with the fully customisable tower PC, but it wasn’t too long ago that mini computers weren’t able to be customised. So, continuing the development of the mini computers, they should have the same opportunity to provide the customisation that desktop computers currently possess.
The best mini PC on the market is the Intel NUC (Next Unit of Computing). Intel has many mini PCs that are at the top of the market. Intel have even created models of their mini PC that can be used to play games. Many mini PCs can do the same tasks as regular PCs and performance has a similar range to the traditional model, whether internet browsing or casual gaming.
Mini PCs are also positive for the environment, as they don’t use as much energy to run as a regular tower PC. They can take on a similar work load and are built in a more durable, robust fashion, which can help extend the life span of the computer.

    What are the main advantages of mini computers?
  • Size, mini PCs save a huge amount of space and with the advancements in technology, can do a similar job compared to a traditional PC.
  • Power consumption, they were created to run on less power than a regular PC.
  • Cost, the cost of a mini PC compared to a traditional PC is miniscule.
    What are the main disadvantages of mini computers?
  • When you buy a mini PC, that is all you get. Other accessories like the keyboard, monitor and mouse will be needed to be purchased separately.
  • There are only certain parts that you can customise, unlike a tower PC which is fully customisable.
  • The RAM is closer to something you would see in a laptop, rather than in a desktop computer. Laptop RAM is smaller and costs a fraction more, but the performance is similar.
  • Gaming is possible; however, it is not recommended on a mini PC, if you are serious about gaming. But, a more casual gamer should be able to use a mini PC with limited issues.
  • If a “barebones” mini PC is bought no operating system is pre-installed. But there are some “non-barebones” mini PCs that don’t come with a pre-installed operating system.


The ability of being able to make hardware smaller has been the major factor of the creation of the mini computer. As the technology improves, it is possible that computers will continue to decrease in size, while not compromising on performance. There has been significant growth in the market for mini pcs, not only due to their price, but also with the improvements of technology as not much performance is being sacrificed by using a mini computer.
With the continued development of the Internet of Things, where the number of devices connected to the internet will be approximately 1.5 billion by 2022, mini PCs like the Raspberry Pi show that we don’t need super computers to do simple, more routine IT tasks. The Raspberry Pi is super versatile, being used in classrooms, libraries, hackspaces, research labs and even in industry environments. The range of possible projects makes the Raspberry Pi perfect for beginners or experts in the computing field. The mini PC allows for the user to “tinker” with the device and be able to continually explore the opportunities of the digital world.

Cyber Security

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks and programs from digital attacks. Everyone benefits from strong cybersecurity, at an individual level and both business and governmental levels. An individual could have their identity stolen or lose personal information and data like family photos or could be blackmailed. Businesses and governments need to protect their large amount of private information.
There are four main technologies that are used in cybersecurity. Hardware authentication, user-behaviour analytics, data loss prevention and deep and machine learning.
“Hardware authentication is an approach to user authentication that relies on a dedicated physical device (such as a token) held by an authorised user, in addition to a basic password, to grant access to computer resources.”
A more secure authentication process is needed than a username and password. As even long and complicated passwords can be hacked. Long and harder to hack passwords are hard to remember.
Multifactor authentication is being used by Intel, with many different ways of logging in are being used in combination with each other to provide more security. Having many steps to log in may be a little extra hassle for a lot of extra security. The combination of the hardware authentication and password creates a two-factor authentication (2FA) system. There is no perfectly secure authentication system. However, two-factor authentication provides an extra layer of security, making the process harder to hack into.
“User-behaviour analytics (UBA) is a set of algorithms that analyse log activity to spot abnormal behaviour, such as repeated login attempts from a single IP address or large file downloads.” Or put simply, “UBA is the tracking, collecting and assessing of user data and activities using monitoring systems”.
It determines a baseline that all activity is compared to – which can help detect deviations from the normal routine of the user. For UBA to work, it relies on packet detection, signature detection, log analysis, advanced analytics and artificial intelligence. UBA takes some time to implement, as there needs to be a big enough dataset to understand the user’s habits online, but it also has to be nuanced enough to understand that there isn’t a breach in security if the user is not following their specific habits. This balance is difficult because you don’t want to be too lenient nor too strict.
Data loss prevention (DLP), monitors all data when it is in transit, at rest and in use.
Data in transit protection – is data in a network (internal or external) that is going to an end destination. DLP tools encrypt the data and use an encrypted transport system like a VPN or SSL to help deliver the data.
Data in use protection – this is data that is used by an application or product. DLP will encrypt the sensitive data and not allow the transmission of the data to any unauthorised storage systems.
Data at rest protection – this is data that is stored in an archive. DLP tools make sure that the whole database is encrypted, but it can be encrypted at many levels: field, table, or the whole database.
DLP looks for attempts from an unauthorised figure or figures that is trying to access the data, to make sure that they cannot get hold of it.
Deep Learning is “a set of techniques for implementing machine learning that recognize patterns of patterns - like image recognition. The systems identify primarily object edges, a structure, an object type, and then an object itself.”
Machine Learning is “an approach to AI that uses a system that is capable of learning from experience… ML is a system that can recognize patterns by using examples rather than by programming them.”
Current trends: Unsupervised Learning. This is a “data driven” approach. It is not as precise as supervised approaches, but it uses the properties of the unlabelled data it is analysing to find abnormalities. This is a powerful approach because it is difficult to categorise data, especially if it is not labelled.
Semi-supervised learning. This technique tries to combine benefits from both supervised and unsupervised approaches, when there are some labelled data.
Future trends: Reinforcement learning. An “environment driven” approach that can be used when the behaviour needs to react to a changing environment. It’s like a kid who is learning environment by trial and error.
Active learning. It’s currently a subclass of reinforcement learning that will eventually grow into its own class. Active learning is like a teacher who can correct both errors and behaviour as well as dealing with environmental changes effectively.
The difficulty of using Machine Learning and Deep Learning is that there needs to be anticipation of how information is going to be stolen. The properties of how the data is going to be taken needs to be decided and then needs to be engineered for the deep learning or machine learning system.
Machine Learning and Deep Learning use the following processes to help protect users from a cyber-attack.
Prediction: it predicts future values based on the information of previous known values.
Classification: separating values into specific categories.
Clustering: a similar action to classification, but the specific categories are unknown, so values with similar attributes are grouped together.
Recommendation: using information from previous values to recommend an action for a future value.
Generalisation: searching for common and important attributes in many values.
Generative models: creating something new based on the information distribution of previous values.

Autonomous Vehicles

An Autonomous Vehicle is a type of transport which is able to operate independently without human input. These systems of transport are currently operating in trains, aircraft and are under development for cars.
The application of the system within trains has been successfully demonstrated over the decades, resulting in maximum efficiency of resources and reliability of operations. Trains with ‘Automatic Train Operation (ATO)’ are able to operate basic functions independently of the driver’s input. The functions consist of accelerating and decelerating while adhering to signals sent from the control center. The human presence is required to act as a safeguard in case of system failure, so that there is always a person who is able to operate the train. There are many large cities which operate fully Autonomous Trains such as Tokyo, Singapore, Hong Kong, London and many more. The implementation of the autonomous system has significantly reduced basic human errors in railway industries.
In the aviation industry, the autonomous system serves as an assistant to the pilots. The system in aviation is more commonly known as the Autopilot, which has the capability of flying the aircraft shortly after take-off, cruise and landing at the destination. The autopilot handles flight components such as airspeed, heading, vertical speed, altitude, engine parameters and countless other systems onboard the aircraft. However, like laptop computers they require constant monitoring and inputs to perform tasks. The sophistication level of the computers does not have authority in situations such as course deviations due to weather obstacles or system failures. The pilot must always manage the autopilot, in which it will endeavour to comply with the instructed commands. Above everything else, the Pilot always has full authority over the computers and is able to override at any moment.
On the ground, automobile companies have been developing and deploying vehicles which have automatic cruise control autopilot which has the ability to travel in a set lane and make switches without any human inputs. The technology is limited to the open highways and is currently unable to operate on heavy-traffic roads. The system is designed to relieve driver stress and fatigue on long journeys on the road, so that when they are approaching difficult and challenging surrounding, they are more alert and able to manipulate the vehicle safely to their destination.
The current state of the technology­­ is constantly under development as the field is relatively new and programmers and engineers are still grappling to make these vehicles safer and more reliable. The market is also adjusting to the introduction of the vehicles, in which it is projected to take some time as customers shift their perspective of the technology.
In the upcoming years to come, the technology which governs automation in vehicles will be significantly improved. Research and Development will always attempt to improve mistakes and boundaries imposed on today’s technology, in the sense that autonomous cars will become predominant in everyday life, efficiency and reduce impact on the environment.
According to a recent article interviewing Elon Musk, the use of Lidar technology is actually not required for a car to sense its surrounding. Instead, a basic camera can serve as a replacement to the expensive Lidar as the car would be also able to perform the same task.
In addition to the physical features of the vehicles, the software is an essential part of the loop which has the responsibility of maintaining safety of passengers and surroundings. The next generation of vehicles would have enhanced software and intelligence which will proceed current generations.
The potential impact of the development will result in a larger amount of Autonomous Vehicle operating with other cars on the road. Constant testing of the technology allows for better understanding and improvements to be made for the technology to become mature for commercial release. The technology intends to improve the quality of life, but the process will most likely create redundancies of occupation from the workforce. The competition offered by the technology is efficiency and effectiveness of services, in which positions such as delivery and personal transport drivers. The traditional role of a human driver at the controls will be redefined with a computer at the helm, however this will not occur for some time because the technology is currently still in its infancy stage.
Although the role of the driver may be likely to be declared as redundant in the future, there also open new opportunities to work within the field. The vehicles require constant maintenance to operate, therefore the need for programmers and mechanics is larger because the demand will be higher. The onboard navigation computer will likely be the feature that require the most frequent updates for better reliability and quality of the vehicle’s travel. Programmers will also be on high demand to tweak performance and efficiency of operations of the vehicles. The end of an era begins for some occupations, though new opportunities will be able to flourish. Robots

A robot by definition is an automated machine which is able to perform tasks and replicate functions automatically. The general term for the machine is that its job is to assist in executing automated functions such as in manufacturing, complex intelligence and much more. There are many applications of robots in the world that takes different forms individually.
In the manufacturing industry, robots perform tasks which humans could not replicate repetitively because of the conditions and the circumstances required for the result. A car-manufacturing facility would employ robots to complete welding frames and secondary quality checking of products. Humans are simply too fallible to conduct tasks like this, in which it makes perfect sense to give robot this task as they are far more productive and improve cost and time efficiency of the production line. Most of the mass-produced cars on the road today including autonomous vehicles have been physically assembled by robots for most of the critical parts and joints, however some tasks are only able to be conducted by human operators.
For robots operating in the complex intelligence field, it does not necessarily mean that they are a part of an Intelligence organization but instead they are more in terms of interacting with people, in which they are able to communicate in natural-language. The natural language is basically common colloquial language, in which the syntax for the robot to understand is quite flexible in contrast to manufacturing counterparts. A great example of this instance is Honda’s ASIMO, who is able to carry a conversation naturally with humans. The market for robots with complex intelligence is still growing, but similar to manufacturing it is seen to have some growth in the not too distant future.
The advancement of robotics technology in today’s era is accelerating at a faster pace, with automated machines having the capability from basic repetitive functions to complex interaction with human beings. Another example of a robot integrating both the simple and complex interaction is Changi Airport’s cleaning robot, who does the basic function of cleaning the floor of the terminal and also able to respond to human obstructions in a polite manner. The robot assists the airport in maintaining a clean environment with efficiency in time and labour; the robotic cleaner can operate with accuracy and report if any arising issues are encountered which may require human intervention.
The next three years of robotics development will take the machine to expand into more fields and purposes. The likelihood of impact on current jobs and occupations would be tremendous if humans were to become immediately redundant by robotics labour. The first stage of robotics job would most likely target the lower levels of occupation, in which people working in this sector would have a higher risk of having their jobs becoming redundant. The timeframe between the era and the present day give people the opportunity to prepare and adapt to the inevitable change.
